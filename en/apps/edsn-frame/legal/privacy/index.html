EDSN Frame Privacy Policy

1. General Provisions

Studio YONA (the "Company") values the personal information of users of the EDSN Frame iOS app (the "Service") and has established this Privacy Policy in order to comply with applicable laws and regulations, including the Personal Information Protection Act.

  • Service name: EDSN Frame
  • Scope of application: iOS app, in-app guest use flow, Apple/Google account linking, in-app purchases, advertising/consent flows, customer support/account deletion flows
  • Operator information: Studio YONA / Representative Nayoung Lee / #419, 4F, 1, Daebangcheon-ro 12-gil, Yeongdeungpo-gu, Seoul, Republic of Korea

2. Items of Personal Information Processed and Purposes of Processing

Category Purpose of Processing Processed Items
Required Providing the Service in guest mode, maintaining app settings, and providing shooting/editing/saving functions Device information (OS version, device model), app version, permission status (camera/photos/tracking), local setting values, local metadata of outputs generated in the app
Optional Linking an Apple or Google account, restoring login sessions, and providing account continuity across devices Apple/Google account identifiers, email address, provider user identifier, internal user ID, and token metadata required for authentication/session handling
Optional In-app purchases, restoration of memberships/ad-skip/shop entitlements, and entitlement synchronization App Store transaction identifiers, product IDs, entitlement IDs, purchase/restoration status, and internal user IDs
Optional Synchronization and account-based restoration of points, achievements, shop ownership, photobooth film, and invite code status Internal user ID, point balance, achievement progress, shop ownership, quantity of photobooth film, and invite code/reward status
Required Prevent repeated fraudulent claiming of referral rewards and free/reward grants Apple DeviceCheck token and verification result, Apple/Google account identifier hash processed with a server-side HMAC secret, installation/device abuse token hash processed with HMAC, reward/grant type, grant key, first/recent detection time, expiration time
Optional Handling customer inquiries, receiving feedback, processing account deletion, and handling complaints Inquiry email address, inquiry contents, app version, iOS version, device model, and any diagnostic summary or screenshots attached by the user
Optional Providing advertisements, measuring ad performance, processing rewarded ads, and managing consent status Advertising identifiers (such as IDFA, when permitted), ad consent status, ad exposure/reward processing results, and ad-skip entitlement status

Additional Notes

  • Based on the current implementation, original photos and videos taken through the Service are generally stored on the user's device or in the user's photo library and are not uploaded to the operator's server during normal use of the Service.
  • Linking an Apple or Google account is optional, and users may start the core shooting flow in guest mode.
  • The Apple DeviceCheck token is sent to Apple to verify and prevent repeated fraudulent claiming of free/reward grants. The Company does not store the raw DeviceCheck token on its server. The Company stores only the minimum records needed for abuse prevention, such as grant status, grant key, and processing time.
  • The Company does not collect unique identifiers such as resident registration numbers, passport numbers, or driver's license numbers.
  • The Company does not, by default, collect sensitive information such as health information or biometric information. If the actual operational scope changes, this Policy will be updated.

4. Procedures and Methods for Destruction of Personal Information

The Company destroys the relevant information without delay when the retention period expires or the purpose of processing has been achieved.

  1. Destruction procedures
    • Information whose retention period has expired or whose purpose has been achieved is destroyed without delay unless there is a separate reason for retention.
    • Where separate retention is required by law, the information is kept separately for the applicable period and then destroyed.
  2. Destruction methods
    • Electronic files are deleted using technical methods that make recovery or reproduction difficult.
    • Printed materials and paper documents are shredded or incinerated.

Hash records used for fraud and abuse prevention do not store original Apple/Google account identifiers, email addresses, device names, or advertising identifiers, and are deleted when the retention period expires.

5. Provision of Personal Information to Third Parties

In principle, the Company does not provide users' personal information to third parties.

However, the following exceptions apply.

  1. Where the user has given separate prior consent
  2. Where there is a special legal basis or a lawful request by an investigative agency or other competent authority
  3. Where information is processed in accordance with the policies of external platforms selected directly by the user, such as Apple, Google, or the App Store

9. Automatic Collection Devices, Behavioral Information Processing, and Methods of Refusal

The Company may process automatically collected information or behavioral information within the scope necessary to provide advertising, measure advertising performance, and operate the Service.

9-1. Processed Items

  • Advertising identifiers (such as IDFA, when permitted by iOS)
  • Apple DeviceCheck token and verification result, used to prevent repeated fraudulent claiming of free/reward grants and not for advertising tracking.
  • Ad exposure/click/reward processing status
  • App version, device OS, permission status, and minimal service usage log information

9-2. Purposes of Processing

  • Serving advertisements and processing ad reward features
  • Measuring advertising performance and preventing fraudulent use
  • Checking service stability

9-3. Methods of Refusal

  1. Refusing iOS tracking
    • Path: Settings > Privacy & Security > Tracking
    • You may disable Allow Apps to Request to Track.
  2. Limiting Apple advertising personalization
    • Path: Settings > Privacy & Security > Apple Advertising
    • You may disable personalized ads.
  3. Changing settings within the app
    • If the Service provides an advertising/consent-related settings screen, you may change your selected state there.

Even if a user refuses, the core shooting features of the Service may continue to be available, but ad-based rewards or some personalization features may be limited.
